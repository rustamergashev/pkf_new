PKF O’Connor Davies' Partner celebrates double award success

We are proud to announce that Dawn Perri, a Partner at our North American member firm PKF O’Connor Davies, has recently received two prestigious accolades. 

Crain’s New York Business, the leading source of news for New York’s business community, has named Dawn as one of its Notable HR Leaders 2022. This award celebrates innovators across the New York metropolitan area who are driving change in their firms’ human resources departments, from revolutionising approaches to acquiring and retaining employees to promoting diversity, equity and inclusion (DE&I).  

914INC, Westchester County’s premier business lifestyle magazine, has selected Dawn as a winner in its Women in Business Awards 2022, an awards programme which shines a spotlight on the area’s most enterprising, influential and inspirational female executives. 

As PKF O’Connor Davies’ Chief Human Resources Officer, Dawn leads human resources for the firm’s 1,400 team members across 18 offices. With over 25 years’ experience, Dawn has been instrumental in developing the firm’s DE&I programme, enhancing bonus and benefits schemes and expanding its recruitment team.  

These latest awards showcase Dawn’s strong leadership, her unrelenting commitment to creating a welcoming and encouraging work environment and her dedication to promoting positive change within the local community. She comments, “My goal has always been to ensure our firm is one of our industry’s top workplaces, where all feel confident, valued and accepted for who they are. These two awards have only strengthened my dedication to that work and have been made possible by the support of our incredible HR team.” 

Kevin J. Keane, Executive Chairman at PKF O’Connor Davies, said, “Dawn is an impactful senior leader and these awards, while impressive and well-deserved, come as no surprise. Her recognition … is a direct reflection of her tireless efforts to improve our culture, grow our team and deepen our commitment to diversity, equity and inclusion.”
